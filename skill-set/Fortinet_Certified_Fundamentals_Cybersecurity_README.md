# Fortinet Certified Fundamentals (FCF) in Cybersecurity

## Overview

The **Fortinet Certified Fundamentals (FCF) in Cybersecurity** is an entry-level certification offered through the Fortinet NSE Training Institute. It is designed to validate foundational knowledge of cybersecurity concepts, the threat landscape, and core security principles. The FCF certification is free and self-paced, making it an accessible starting point for students, career changers, and IT professionals beginning their journey into cybersecurity. It also serves as a stepping stone toward Fortinet's more advanced Network Security Expert (NSE) certification levels.

The FCF certification is earned by completing a series of foundational courses and passing their associated assessments.

---

## Who This Certification Is For

- Students and recent graduates exploring cybersecurity careers
- IT professionals transitioning into security roles
- Career changers with little to no prior cybersecurity background
- Anyone seeking a recognized, no-cost credential to begin building a cybersecurity resume

## Prerequisites

- No prior cybersecurity experience required
- Basic computer and internet literacy is helpful
- No cost to enroll or to earn the certification

---

## Learning Objectives

By completing the FCF certification path, learners will be able to:

1. Explain foundational cybersecurity concepts and terminology
2. Describe the current cyber threat landscape and common attack types
3. Identify cybersecurity career paths, roles, and required skills
4. Understand core network security principles and architectures
5. Recognize key security technologies and how they defend against threats
6. Apply foundational best practices for protecting data, systems, and networks
7. Understand Fortinet's role and product ecosystem within the security industry

---

## Certification Outline (Course Path)

The FCF certification is composed of multiple foundational courses, each with its own knowledge checks and final assessment. Typical courses in the path include:

### 1. Getting Started in Cybersecurity
- What cybersecurity is and why it matters
- Cybersecurity career paths and required skills
- How security teams are structured and operate
- Building a roadmap for a cybersecurity career

### 2. Introduction to the Threat Landscape
- Evolution of the modern threat landscape
- Threat actors, motivations, and attack techniques
- The cyber kill chain and attack lifecycle
- Best practices for defending against common threats

### 3. Network Security Fundamentals
- Core networking concepts relevant to security (TCP/IP, ports, protocols)
- Network security architecture and segmentation
- Firewalls, VPNs, and perimeter defense concepts
- Introduction to Zero Trust principles

### 4. Introduction to Security Operations
- Overview of Security Operations Centers (SOCs)
- Security monitoring, detection, and incident response basics
- The role of SIEM and security analytics tools
- Common SOC workflows and escalation processes

### 5. Cybersecurity for Business Leaders *(if included in current path)*
- Communicating cyber risk to leadership and stakeholders
- Aligning security strategy with business objectives
- Compliance, governance, and risk management basics

> **Note:** Fortinet periodically updates the specific courses included in the FCF path. Learners should confirm the current course lineup on the Fortinet Training Institute portal, as titles and number of modules may change.

---

## Tools and Materials

- Fortinet NSE Training Institute platform (free account required)
- Self-paced video lessons, readings, and interactive content
- Knowledge checks after each module/course
- No software installation or lab environment required

---

## Assessment and Certification

- Each course includes module-level knowledge checks
- A final assessment/exam is required to complete each course
- Completing all required courses in the path awards the **FCF Certificate**
- Digital badge issued upon successful completion, shareable on LinkedIn and professional profiles

---

## Estimated Duration

Approximately **4–8 hours total**, self-paced, depending on the number of courses included in the current FCF path.

---

## Next Steps After Certification

- Progress to **Fortinet NSE 1–3** certifications for more applied security awareness training
- Pursue technical NSE certifications (NSE 4+) for hands-on FortiGate and security product training
- Use the FCF credential as a foundation for entry-level cybersecurity job applications
