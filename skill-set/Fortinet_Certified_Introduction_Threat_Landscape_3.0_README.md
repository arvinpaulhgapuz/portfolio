# Fortinet – Introduction to the Threat Landscape 3.0

## Overview

This course is part of the **Fortinet Certified Fundamentals (FCF) in Cybersecurity** training track and provides a foundational understanding of today's cybersecurity threat landscape. Learners are introduced to the types of threats organizations face, the motivations and tactics of threat actors, and the core security concepts and terminology needed to begin a career in cybersecurity. The course emphasizes awareness and comprehension over hands-on configuration, making it an ideal entry point for those new to the field.

---

## Who This Course Is For

- Individuals new to cybersecurity with little to no prior experience
- IT professionals seeking foundational security knowledge
- Students pursuing the Fortinet Certified Fundamentals (FCF) certification
- Anyone wanting a broad understanding of cyber threats and security terminology

## Prerequisites

- No prior cybersecurity experience required
- Basic computer literacy and general familiarity with the internet is helpful

---

## Learning Objectives

By completing this course, learners will be able to:

1. Define key cybersecurity terms and concepts
2. Describe the evolution of the modern threat landscape
3. Identify common types of cyber threats and attack vectors
4. Explain the motivations and profiles of different threat actors
5. Recognize common attack techniques, including malware, phishing, and social engineering
6. Understand the basics of the cyber kill chain and attack lifecycle
7. Describe the role of security solutions in defending against threats
8. Identify best practices for protecting individuals and organizations from cyber threats

---

## Course Outline

### Module 1: Introduction to Cybersecurity
- What is cybersecurity, and why does it matter?
- Key terminology: vulnerability, exploit, threat, risk
- The CIA triad: Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability
- Overview of the cybersecurity industry and career paths

### Module 2: The Evolving Threat Landscape
- History and evolution of cyber threats
- Trends driving the modern threat landscape (cloud, IoT, remote work)
- The expanding attack surface
- Notable real-world cyberattacks and their impact

### Module 3: Threat Actors and Motivations
- Types of threat actors: cybercriminals, hacktivists, nation-states, insider threats
- Understanding attacker motivations (financial, political, ideological)
- The cybercrime economy and Cybercrime-as-a-Service (CaaS)
- Advanced Persistent Threats (APTs)

### Module 4: Common Threats and Attack Techniques
- Malware types: viruses, worms, ransomware, trojans, spyware
- Phishing and social engineering tactics
- Denial-of-Service (DoS) and Distributed Denial-of-Service (DDoS) attacks
- Man-in-the-middle attacks and credential theft
- Zero-day vulnerabilities and exploits

### Module 5: The Cyber Kill Chain and Attack Lifecycle
- Stages of the cyber kill chain (reconnaissance through exfiltration)
- How attackers plan and execute campaigns
- MITRE ATT&CK framework overview
- Why understanding the attack lifecycle matters for defense

### Module 6: Defending Against Threats
- Core security principles: defense in depth, least privilege
- Overview of common security solutions (firewalls, endpoint protection, SIEM)
- The role of security awareness and training
- Introduction to Fortinet's Security Fabric approach

### Module 7: Best Practices and Risk Reduction
- Practical steps for individuals and organizations
- Password hygiene, multi-factor authentication, and patch management
- Incident reporting and response basics
- Building a security-first mindset
